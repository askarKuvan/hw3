How to survive Baylor's Computer Science Master Program

3 Tips for Survival

Posted by Askar Kuvanychbekov on July 23, 2019
Image from populous.com

If you are planning to do computer science Master's program in Baylor or if you just started the program, then you better be prepared. Being computer science grad student in Baylor is not easy. You need to literally survive it.

Here are three tips that will help you survive based on experience of current grad students.

1. Forget about personal life

There's no such a thing as a free lunch. You're slave now. You will have tons of assignments waiting for your grading everyweek.

Not mentioning course projects, quizzes, paper summaries, exams, reading assignments, writing assignments, programming assignments, all sort of assignments that professor can come up with to torture you

You will be happy when you find time to eat.

You can forget about going out with friends, parties and all sort of fun stuff.

2. Forget about sleep

As you might guessed, there will no time for sleep too. It's because sleep is for the weak, and computer science graduate students in Baylor are not weak.

Sleep is for the weak (image taken from youtube).

3. Be prepared to live in grad lab

You will be paying rent for your apartment just because you need a place to store your belongings, because you will be living in grad lab, in computer science student's natural habitat.
